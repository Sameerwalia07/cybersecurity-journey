## 🎯 What I Learned

- Defensive security covers two core goals: **preventing** cyber attacks and
  **detecting** them when prevention fails, while minimising their impact on
  systems and the business
- The Blue Team is the umbrella term for teams working on defensive security
- Key functions within defensive security: SOC, Digital Forensics, Threat
  Intelligence, Incident Response, and Malware Analysis

## 🧠 In My Own Words

Defensive security isn't just about stopping attacks before they happen — it's
just as much about catching attacks that get through and limiting the damage
they cause. These two goals, prevention and detection, work together rather
than replacing each other.

The **Blue Team** is the overall name for the people doing this work, and it
splits into a few specialised functions:

- **SOC (Security Operations Center):** a team that continuously watches the
  network for suspicious or malicious activity, essentially acting as the
  eyes on the system at all times
- **Threat Intelligence:** the practice of researching and understanding
  attackers — their methods, tools, and targets — so an organisation can
  strengthen its defenses *before* those specific threats show up
- **Digital Forensics:** the investigative side — using tools and techniques
  to figure out exactly what happened during an attack, how it happened, and
  what was affected, usually after the fact
- **Incident Response:** the active, in-the-moment work of containing an
  attack while it's still happening, aiming to stop it from escalating into
  a major breach
- **Malware Analysis:** examining malicious files, documents, or programs to
  understand what they do — this can be done two ways: **static analysis**
  (examining the file without running it) and **dynamic analysis** (running
  it in a safe, isolated environment to observe its behavior)

What stood out to me is how these functions form a kind of timeline: threat
intelligence works *before* an attack, SOC and incident response work
*during*, and forensics and malware analysis often work *during and after* to
understand the full picture.
