## 🧠 In My Own Words

Cybersecurity is a world of constantly evolving threats, where a single
click can hide an attack, and a single attack can seriously damage a
business. This is exactly why defenders are needed — people who analyse
attacks, stop breaches, and keep organisations safe.

As a **Junior Security Analyst** (also called a **SOC Level 1 Analyst**),
you work within a **24/7 SOC team**, mostly reviewing security alerts
alongside colleagues. Typical responsibilities include:
- Monitoring and investigating various security alerts
- Participating in SOC brainstorms and workshops
- Cooperating with other teams to keep the company safe
- Constantly learning about new cyber threats and defences

### The wider SOC team structure

- **Senior Analyst** — supports junior analysts when something's unclear,
  and takes over complex cases after the junior analyst has done the
  initial analysis
- **SOC Engineer** — maintains the security tools and configures alerts,
  making the analyst's job easier and more effective
- **SOC Manager** — keeps overall operations under control, reports SOC
  results to higher authority, and helps ensure the team doesn't get lost
  navigating the constantly changing threat landscape
- **Incident Responder** — called in on demand specifically when a major
  incident occurs, handling the escalated, high-severity response

### Example daily duties of a Junior Analyst

- Detecting and preventing a data stealer infection on a coworker's laptop
- Analysing and stopping a phishing campaign targeting the finance team
- Participating in a larger incident, such as a full-scale ransomware attack
- Teaming up with teammates to build detection rules and automations
- Understanding how the company operates beyond just the technical side, to
  better judge what's actually at risk

## 🛠️ Key Terms Introduced

- Junior Security Analyst / SOC Level 1 Analyst
- SOC team roles: Senior Analyst, SOC Engineer, SOC Manager, Incident Responder
